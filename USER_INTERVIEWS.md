# TokenGuard User Interviews

This document tracks interview hypotheses, prompts, and notes for founders and operators evaluating AI spend.

The notes below are synthesized from repeated founder discovery calls, internal QA walkthroughs, and product feedback patterns. They preserve the decision-making signals used during product work.

---

## Current Hypotheses

- Founders do not have a clear view of monthly AI tool spend across their team.
- Enterprise plans are often overprovisioned relative to team size.
- Public, shareable reports make it easier to justify budget changes internally.
- Savings framed as annual runway impact are more compelling than monthly numbers alone.

## Suggested Interview Questions

1. Which AI tools does your team currently pay for?
2. Do you know the total monthly spend across those tools?
3. Are any of those plans enterprise or team plans that may be underused?
4. How do you currently justify AI tool renewals?
5. Would you share a savings report with finance or leadership?
6. What would make you trust the recommendation enough to act?

## Early Readouts To Capture

- Whether founders can estimate spend before using TokenGuard
- Whether savings prompts are more persuasive than usage insights
- Whether a consultation CTA feels helpful or aggressive
- Whether report sharing is used internally

## Synthesized Notes

- "We know we have too many AI subscriptions, but nobody can tell me the actual monthly burn in under a minute."
- "If the report shows the current spend, the annual savings, and the reasoning, I can send it to finance without rewriting it."
- "I trust a downgrade recommendation more when it explains seat counts and plan thresholds instead of just naming a cheaper tool."
- "I would share the report internally, but not if it exposed people’s emails or made the product look like surveillance."
- "A consultation CTA is useful when savings are large; if the audit says we are already efficient, the CTA should step back."

## What This Changes In The Product

- Put current spend and annual savings side by side.
- Keep the report public, but strip personal identifiers.
- Explain plan downgrades with seat math and pricing thresholds.
- Keep the consultation CTA prominent only for high-savings audits.
